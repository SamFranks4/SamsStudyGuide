Core Economics Basics

Economics The study of how a society uses scarce resources to produce and distribute goods and services.
Economic system The way a government decides what gets produced and how it’s distributed.
Capitalism An economic system based on private ownership and competition.
Communism An economic system where the government owns and controls almost everything.
Socialism An economic system where major industries are owned or heavily controlled by the government.
Mixed economies Economies that combine private ownership with government involvement.

Business & Organizations

Business An organization that seeks profit by providing goods or services.
Not-for-profit organization An organization focused on goals other than profit.
Entrepreneurs People who combine resources to create goods or services for profit.
Strategic alliance A cooperative agreement between businesses.
Relationship management Building long-term relationships with customers and partners.

Production & Resources

Factors of production Resources used to make goods and services.
Capital Tools, machinery, and buildings used in production.
Knowledge The skills and talents of the workforce.
Knowledge workers Workers who create and apply knowledge.
Productivity How much output one worker can produce.

Markets & Competition

Market structure The number of suppliers in a market.
Perfect competition Many small firms, easy entry, similar products.
Monopolistic competition Many firms selling similar but slightly different products.
Oligopoly A few firms dominate the market.
Pure monopoly One firm controls the entire market.
Barriers to entry Factors that prevent new firms from entering a market.

Supply, Demand & Prices

Demand How much people are willing to buy at different prices.
Demand curve A graph showing quantity demanded at various prices.
Equilibrium Where quantity supplied equals quantity demanded.
Costs Expenses from producing goods or services.
Revenue Money earned from sales.
Profit Money left after all costs are paid.

Economic Performance

Gross domestic product (GDP) Total value of goods and services produced in a country yearly.
Economic growth An increase in a nation’s output.
Business cycles Up-and-down changes in economic activity.
Recession A decline in GDP for at least two quarters.
Full employment Everyone who wants to work has a job.

Inflation & Prices

Inflation A rise in overall price levels.
Consumer Price Index (CPI) Measures prices paid by consumers.
Producer Price Index (PPI) Measures prices paid by producers.
Cost-push inflation Inflation caused by rising production costs.
Demand-pull inflation Inflation caused by high demand.
Purchasing power What money can actually buy.

Government & Policy

Fiscal policy Government taxing and spending decisions.
Monetary policy Government control of money supply and interest rates.
Expansionary policy Policy that increases the money supply.
Contractionary policy Policy that tightens the money supply.
Federal Reserve System (the Fed) The U.S. central banking system.
Federal budget deficit When government spends more than it collects.
National debt Total of all past federal deficits.
Crowding out Government spending replacing private spending.
Savings bonds Small-denomination government bonds.

Unemployment Types

Frictional unemployment Short-term unemployment between jobs.
Cyclical unemployment Unemployment caused by economic downturns.
Seasonal unemployment Unemployment tied to certain seasons.

Goods, Services & Living Standards

Goods Tangible products you can touch.
Services Intangible products you can’t touch or store.
Standard of living What people can buy with their income.
Quality of life Overall well-being and happiness.

Big-Picture Economics

Macroeconomics The study of the economy as a whole.
Microeconomics The study of individual households and firms.
Circular flow Movement of money and resources between households, businesses, and government.
Demography The study of population statistics.
Risk The chance of losing time or money.
